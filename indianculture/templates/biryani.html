BIRYANI

History

Hyderabad Biryani has its roots in the kitchens of the Nizams, who were known for their lavish and diverse culinary traditions. The dish is believed to have been created in the kitchens of the Nizam rulers, combining Mughlai and Iranian flavors with local spices and ingredients. Over time, it became a symbol of the rich culinary heritage of Hyderabad

Preparation:

The preparation of Hyderabad Biryani is an elaborate process that involves marinating meat, partially cooking the rice, and then layering the meat and rice in a heavy-bottomed pot. The pot is then sealed with dough to trap the steam, allowing the flavors to infuse and the meat to cook tenderly. The slow-cooking method, known as "dum pukht," results in a biryani with distinct layers of aromatic rice and flavorful meat.
